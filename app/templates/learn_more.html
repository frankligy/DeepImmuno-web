DeepImmuno

Prediction of epitope immunogenicity for CD8+ Cell

How to interpret immunogenicity score?

A high immunogenicty score means we are more confident about this peptide-HLA complex will trigger T cell response, compared to a one with lower immunogenicity score. It doesn't necessarily reflect the actual degree of immunogenicity for a selected peptide, the reason is we believe it is always biased to conclude whether a peptide would be immunogenic or not solely based on HLA-peptide information, without TCR sequence input.

To further understand why the immunogenicity score reflects the confidence, It relats to how we define the immunogenicity potential, we reason that a if a peptide1 was tested 40 times and responded 40 times should hold a higher confidence than peptide2, which was tested 6 times and only responded 1 times. We used a beta-binomial model to incorporate this layer of information to derive a immunogenicity potential used for training. So the output value can reflect our confidence given the best current knowledge we have.

When should I check binding affnity option?

It is always good to check it for a comprehensive understanding of the results, however, the only downside of checking it is that, it will requires addtional computing time (30-60s) because we are calling an external package mhcflurry. A successful CD8+ immune response requires two step: (1) First the peptide has to be presented by HLA allele, (2) Then the HLA-peptide complex need to be able to trigger T cell response. In our training dataset, all the immunogenic peptides are HLA-bound, but for some of the unseen HLA-peptide complex, DeepImmuno-CNN sometimes may generate a high immunogenicity score even though this peptide is very likely not able to bind with HLA allele. In order to avoid this problem, checking binding affnity can give you a better idea regarding how to interprete the result.

Why there's a Caveat beside my queried HLA allele?

Because the HLA you queried, based on our investigation, may generate unreliable result since this HLA allele is less-studied and we do not have enough training data instance to reliably infer its immunogenecity.
